## Strongest Claims (Priority Order)

**Based on: Case file analysis + 2024 Alberta case law research**

### 1. Family Status Discrimination (STRONGEST - $25k-$40k, NON-TAXABLE)
**What:** Terminated for childcare lateness 12 days after returning from 5-week parental leave. Boss withdrew 9 months of accommodation after employee asserted family boundary (July 20).

**Why It's #1:**
- **Recent Alberta cases (2024): $25k-$40k for similar facts** (Silliker v Suncor: $40k + wages, Bauknecht: $35k + wages)
- **NON-TAXABLE damages** (saves $7k-$12k in taxes vs. lost wages)
- **12-day timing = presumption of discrimination** under Alberta law
- **7 smoking guns** with direct evidence (not circumstantial)
- 1-year limitation period creates employer urgency

**Smoking Guns:**
- Boss (July 21): **"When we're in crunch time, I need you in the shop"**
- HR Michelle (July/Aug): **"Everyone here has had children and no one else is late. Having children should not be a reason"**
- Aug 28: Sick baby denied, doctor's note required
- Oct 10: Return from parental leave, Write-Up #4 issued SAME DAY (33-day delay proves retaliation)
- Oct 22: Terminated **12 days** after protected leave return

**Evidence:** Text messages, Michelle's statement, objective timeline, doctor's note

### 2. Disability Discrimination (STRONG SECONDARY - $25k-$50k, NON-TAXABLE)
**What:** Terminated for lateness/timecard issues related to TWO documented disabilities (ADHD + sleep apnea) with ZERO accommodation provided.

**Why It's #2:**
- **Recent Alberta case: $50k for ADHD + PTSD** (EZ Motors, Red Deer)
- **NON-TAXABLE damages** (huge tax advantage)
- **Boss has BOTH same conditions** (eliminates "undue hardship" defense)
- Employer had full knowledge (doctor's notes on file since day 1 for ADHD, Feb 2025 for sleep apnea)
- Disciplined for disability-related behaviors with no accommodation

**Strategic Value:**
- Hard for employer to defeat BOTH family status AND disability claims
- Creates alternative legal theory (if one fails, other may succeed)
- Increases settlement pressure (multiple protected grounds)

**Evidence:** Pre-employment drug test (ADHD medication), doctor's notes (both conditions), boss's admission "I understand the struggles"

### 3. Wrongful Dismissal + Bad Faith (FOUNDATION - $15k-$35k)
**What:** Just cause threshold not met (high bar in Alberta). Progressive discipline was coercive (10+ termination threats), inconsistent application, signed under duress.

**Damages Breakdown:**
- Wrongful dismissal: $10k-$15k (2-3 months notice, TAXABLE)
- Bad faith conduct: +$5k-$20k (Wallace damages, aggravating factors)

**Bad Faith Pattern:**
- ROE delayed 9-14 days (2024 case: $10k punitive for similar delay)
- Email access cut while reviewing documents (spoliation)
- 10+ termination threats throughout employment
- Differential sick note treatment (Jessica vs. employee, final week)

**Evidence:** Termination timing (12 days post-leave), ROE delay emails, email cutoff, differential treatment

### 4. Contract Error - Tactical Credibility Destroyer (LEVERAGE TOOL, not standalone claim)
**What:** Employment contract addressed to **"Dear Rollins:"** (wrong employee name).

**Strategic Use:**
- **Opens settlement negotiation:** "They can't even get my name right - how can we trust their evidence?"
- Undermines credibility of ALL employer documentation (write-ups, timecards, termination reasons)
- May void contract ‚Üí termination clause doesn't apply (+$10k-$20k if proven)
- Creates lawyer embarrassment (Carol Burke sent the document)

**How to Use:**
- Lead with this to destroy employer credibility FIRST
- Then present family status + disability claims with full force
- But settle based on human rights damages value, not contract error alone

**Evidence:** PDF contract in `evidence/Doc from Carol/`, undeniable error visible
